Dear Disha,

I’m writing this because I just wanted you to know the truth. When we argued that night on call, I was drunk and completely out of my consciousness. I couldn’t think straight, I couldn’t explain myself, and I started panicking and flooding your phone with messages. I understand why you blocked me.

When you called me fake, it broke me, but looking back I understand exactly why you said it. I want to explain my side, not to make excuses, but so you know the real story.

First, the lie about my past. When we first started dating, I told you I had an ex who cheated on me. The reality was she wasn’t an official ex, she was a girl I was actively dating. She went behind my back and I found out from someone else. It hurt a lot. I changed the story to make it simple. I confessed recently because I wanted real transparency between you and me, but the timing made everything worse.
The mirror selfie lie. When you asked for the picture, my room was a complete mess. Instead of just saying I felt self-conscious, I lied and said I didn’t have a big mirror. When you saw the mirror later, it made me look like a habitual liar. It was nothing but my insecurity.

After our first breakup I was really hurt and angry. That anger changed me and I became bitter and rude to others. When you came back with genuine feelings, I stupidly brought that same rudeness to you. My ego was scared of getting hurt again. It wasn’t fair to you at all.

About the keychain. When I mentioned the printing cost me 800rs, I got distracted and didn’t explain the rest. The shop scammed me, they charged A3 rate instead of A4 which was comparatively cheaper because I was in a hurry. I was just venting about getting scammed. I never meant to make it sound like I was keeping score of how much i spent on you. I’m so sorry for how that came across.

I don’t expect you to read this and forget everything. I know I damaged your trust badly. I’m disappointed in myself. I’m actively working on stopping the lying habit and killing my ego. I won’t push you, bother you, or try to bypass your block. You’ll see through my actions that I mean it.

Whenever you are ready to talk, I am here.

Truly sorry,
The guy who still cares a lot
